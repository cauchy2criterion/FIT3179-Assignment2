The State of Renewable Energy

Renewable energy is energy that can be generated from naturally replenishable sources in a timely manner. Now that we are facing an environmental crisis with our dependence on imported fuels, renewable energy is seen as a reliable alternative that will not only diversify our energy supply but also generate clean energy that produces no or little greenhouse gas emissions. It is predicted that renewable energy will play a prominent role in achieving a sustainable future by conserving our natural resources and allow for an economic development for jobs to thrive in.

How is Renewable Energy in Australia?

Let us take a look at Australia's states. This map displays the total percentage of renewable energy from the total energy generated throughout 2008 to 2019.

Tasmania's renewable energy generation is 98.14%, making it the most renewable state in 2019 (and of all time).

Northern Territory's renewable energy production is 4.20%. This means that it is the least renewable state in 2019.

What are the different energy sources and how much do they provide?

Renewable energy consists of Biofuel, Hydropower, Nuclear Power, Wind, Solar Power and other renewable sources that are have not been identified.

Renewable energy usage is measured in TWh (terrawatt-hour). TWh is a standardised measure which ensures that comparisons between energy sources are consistent.

The line in the chart measures the mean. You can drag in the chart to find means between specific sources.

Non-renewable energy is still the main provider of energy. These consist of fossil fuels, oil, coal, gas and nuclear. By far, energy generated by fossil fuels are the most widely used.

Nuclear energy is also under non-renewable. The materials used for nuclear fission (mainly uranium) is considered non-renewable, although there are renewable alternatives.

Renewable and Non-Renewable Energy over the years

With the rising awareness for the need to switch to clean energy we expect renewable energy sources to grow as the years go by. But it seems that the demand for energy from fossil fuels are also growing.

Hopefully, in the future we will see renewable energy being a reliable energy provider and replace non-renewable energy sources.

Is Renewable Energy the solution to Carbon Emissions?

It is essential to understand that renewable energy will not resolve the all greenhouse gas emissions problem. There are a lot of complex factors that contribute to the greenhouse gas emissions like how we use energy.
For instance, Norway is mostly renewable, yet they produce more CO2 per million people than most countries.

Note that CO2 Emission is a logarithmic scale.

Why Renewable Energy? What's the rush?

As we can see, the average temperature has been rising. This is largely attributed to CO2 emissions from non-renewable energy sources. Rising temperatures can lead to rising sea-levels, climate change and risk of many other natural predicaments.

Renewable energy is a step in lowering CO2 emissions and will hopefully aid in resolving the environmental crisis.
